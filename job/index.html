I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Complex Network Research in Northeastern University Networks Science Institute. Prior to joining CCNR, I completed my Ph.D. at Indiana University, Bloomington. My dissertation, titled as "Analyzing Social Big Data to Study Online Discourse and its Manipulation", is awarded 2018 University Distinguished Ph.D. Dissertation Award.

I'm on the job market now.

Brief descriptions of my research projects, list of publications, and educational background can be found from the links.

I am broadly interested in topics on computational social science.

My research focuses on developing techniques to analyze online behaviors to improve individual well-being and address societal problems using online data.

My research on socio-technological systems bring opportunities to study cultural patterns, dynamics of emotions, and collective phenomena. Information collected through various services and devices tell stories in macro- and micro-scale.

My long-term research goal is developing models that describe intents and actions of individuals and groups using machine learning, network science, and computational social sciences.
